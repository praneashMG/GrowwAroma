A Journey to Grasse:
The Rose Harvest

By Guillaume Laurent
October 24, 2024 5 Min Read

The air in Grasse during the month of May is unlike anywhere else on earth. It is thick, honeyed, and heavy with the intoxicating aroma of the Centifolia rose—the undisputed queen of perfumery.

For generations, the hillsides of southern France have been the cradle of modern fragrance. It is here that our story begins for the newest addition to the ScentAura Private Reserve. We traveled to the historic fields just before dawn, the precise moment when the rose petals are at their most fragrant, before the Mediterranean sun evaporates their precious oils.

The Art of the Harvest

Harvesting the Centifolia rose is a painstaking labor of love. The flowers must be picked entirely by hand. The harvesters move swiftly and silently through the rows, gently snapping the blossom just below the calyx. By mid-morning, the harvest must be complete, and the burlap sacks filled with pink blooms are rushed immediately to the extraction facility.

"A fragrance is not just a scent; it is a captured memory, immortalized in glass. The Grasse rose brings a heartbeat to the perfume."

It takes approximately 300,000 individual roses—roughly three tonnes of flowers—to produce a single kilogram of absolute. This astonishing ratio is exactly why the true Grasse rose is reserved exclusively for the world's most luxurious formulations. It possesses a depth, a spicy facet, and a honeyed warmth that synthetic alternatives simply cannot replicate.

From Field to Flacon

Once the absolute is secured, it is transported back to our Parisian laboratory. Here, it is blended with deep, resonant notes of Indonesian Oud and Madagascan Vanilla to create a striking contrast between the delicate floral and the dark woods. The result is a scent that is both romantic and dangerous—a modern interpretation of a classic ingredient.

Wearing a fragrance crafted with Grasse rose is to wear a piece of history. It is an olfactory tribute to the artisans, the farmers, and the sun-drenched earth of the French Riviera.
